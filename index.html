Proposal

This page is intended to act as a toolkit-generator for producing structures of aesthetic encounter with the contours of prediction and reconstruction shaping urban spaces. Organized around a set of prompts and questions, it is intended as a script for contemplation and production of a new vocabulary to engage with the timeliness of urban conditions.

The project proceeds from a few initial starting points.

1. The Mode of Proposal

The project proceeds from an understanding of the proposal, even if unrealized as an important tool for the expansion of civic imagination to be deployed in conjunction with forms of direct action. I take language to be a central starting point for generating forms beyond what is immediately possible – an apt tool for a moment of collective uncertainty. An invitation of this sort emerges in distinction to present conditions of emergency, and uncertainty. This is understood in dialogue with tools for expanding the roles of artistic and architectural education, and efforts to democratize its access. In this sense it is a syllabus, lesson plan, assignment, or script for collaborative structure.

2. Prediction–Reconstruction Complex

I am concerned more narrowly in interfacing with the conditions which generate visualizations of possible futures or reconstructions of previous historical moments.

3. A City Which Feels You

A Media Lab City Science 2017 article begins with the question: “Can you imagine a city that feels, understands, and cares about your wellbeing?” It outlines a framework for future urban development comprised of layers of caring, understanding, and feeling. It describes a city that is at once smart, sensitive and caring – one which uses psychological tools to re-educate its population. Models of perceptual and emotional feedback are utilized to automate civic participation. I am concerned with this slippage, an uncanny transference of the psychological to the architectural.

4. A History of Emotions

The history of emotions is an emerging academic field which traces and analyses the changing norms and rules of feeling. It looks at the development and organization of emotional regimes, codes, and lexicons. This project seeks to affirm the contingent nature of emotion and its discrete manifestations manifestation.
